Creating a Modern
Store Website Builder

Role: Shopify UX Research and Design Intern

Timeline: Fall 2019

Tools: Figma and Origami Studio

Context

In the Fall of 2019, I worked on the Online Store team at Shopify. We were responsible for providing our merchants everything they need to start an online store and sell online in minutes. Specifically, we oversaw a website design tool that empowered business owners to seamlessly design, maintain and update the look and feel of their storefront.

Foreword

The work from this project has yet to be released, but is set to ship sometime in 2020. Due to the nature of Shopify being a publicly traded company and the highly sensitive nature of this project, I cannot publicly share my designs and process yet. However, I’d be happy to discuss this project in more detail through email at daniell.yang@uwaterloo.ca.

Problem

Originally released in 2010, Shopify’s Theme Editor was cutting edge product at the time. It allowed for both custom-coded store sites and no-code sites; for those that were less technically adept, Shopify provided a template design (themes) marketplace with both free and paid options, which merchants could then modify to easily get an online store up and running with 0 coding experience.

However, since then the no-code movement has evolved leaps and bounds. In particular, our competitors Squarespace and Webflow were giving their users almost infinite creative freedom with their seamless drag and drop interfaces.

Contrasting, in the eyes of our merchants, our site builder’s no-code paradigm of modifying a pre-designed template felt restrictive and unoriginal in 2019. It did not have full drag and drop design capabilities, draft changes or content portability when a merchant wanted to change a theme.

Solution

Since we are always striving to make commerce better and empower our merchants. Listening to their feedback, one of Shopify’s top priorities for 2020 was to ship a ground-up rebuild of the store website design tool. It would feature modern capabilities—such as a full drag and drop interface, a drafts workspace and content portability between themes—to compete with our competitors.

Specifically, I led user research and design on a core feature that allowed merchants to seamlessly integrate third-party storefront design apps—think product review apps and promotional pop-up apps—onto their storefronts with no code required. This project provided me a tremendous learning opportunity to leverage user research to make informed design decisions, information architecture to establish educational mental models and intuitive design tooling to simplify workflows.
