Creating a Modern
Store Website Builder

Role: Shopify UX Research and Design Intern

Timeline: Fall 2019

Tools: Figma and Origami Studio

Context

In the Fall of 2019, I worked on the Online Store Editor team at Shopify. We were responsible for providing our merchants everything they need to start an online store and sell online in minutes. Specifically, we oversaw a website design tool that empowered business owners to seamlessly design, maintain and update the look and feel of their storefront.

Foreword

The work from this project has yet to be released, but is set to ship sometime in 2020. Thus, due to the nature of Shopify being a publicly traded company and the highly sensitive nature of this project, I cannot publicly share any assets yet. However, I’d be happy to discuss in more detail my process through email at daniell.yang@uwaterloo.ca.

Problem

Originally released in 2010, it was a cutting edge product for its time; Shopify provided template designs (themes) which prospective entrepreneurs could then modify to easily get an online store up and running with 0 coding required. However, our competitors Squarespace and Webflow were pushing the envelope in 2019, giving their users almost infinite creative freedom with their seamless drag and drop no-code interfaces.

On the other hand, our site builder's current paradigm of only letting our merchants modify a template was beginning to show its age. It did not have full drag and drop design capabilities, draft changes and content portability when a merchant wanted to change a theme. All this resulted in the tool being seen as too restrictive and out-dated in the eyes of our merchants.

Solution

Our goal for 2020 was to ship a full ground-up rebuild of the online store site design tool. It would feature modern capabilities—such as a full drag and drop interface, a drafts workspace and content portability between themes—to compete with competitors like Squarespace.

Specifically, I ran user research and design on a core feature which allowed our merchants to seamlessly integrate third-party apps onto their storefronts with no code required. This project gave me the opportunity to dive deep into the workflow of our merchants, information architecture and the world of design tooling to simplify our merchants lives and contribute to the no-code movement..
