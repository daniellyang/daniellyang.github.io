Redesigning Shopify's mobile marketing experience

Shopify - Winter 2019

Context

A designer's best work comes through collaboration

During my time at Shopify I worked under the Marketing Technology team, handling the marketing section. Launching in late 2018, our team's mission was to empower our merchants to be able to market on a variety of social channels, such as Facebook and Google, all within the Shopify admin, in turn, helping them take their small businesses to the next level.

This was a 12 week project that I championed as the lead product designer. I worked closely with a product manager, a data scientist, and an Android develper and an iOS developer.

Problem space

Transforming a web view into a mobile native design

Since the marketing section was a new product feature, the first iteration within the mobile app was only an MVP. The marketing campaign creation forms were simply web-views shrunk down. Thus, the design lacked consistency with the rest of the native mobile experience and did not leverage the strengths of the mobile platform. All this culminated in a clunky and merchant-hostile experience.

Business opportunity

Emerging markets outside of North America are mobile-first

The future of Shopify's business is in emerging markets, where smartphones are much more accessible than desktops. In fact, one of Shopify's key markets for expansion is Asia, where mobile usage dominates the market; thus, it was crucial to deliver a great mobile user experience for these target markets.


Goals

  1. Revamp the experience to a fully native mobile experience that adhered to Shopify's Polaris design system

  2. Create an experience that is friendly to both first time and repeat users of Facebook carousel ads

  3. Design components that were scaleable and robust enough for future complex marketing campaigns



Research

Drawing inspiration from Airbnb's frictionless form pages

I began by researching form page designs and landed on Airbnb's host set up form. I was in awe of how the team there was able to create such a frictionless and goal-oriented experience for something users normally dread and find mundane.

Some key takeaways from researching Airbnb's form page designs:

  • Decoupled form pages helped to establish a journey for the user to embark upon, making the purpose of each form page clear
  • An immediate and clear goal on each form page keeps the user focused and creates the experience of a quest rather than a chore
  • The progress bar on the header informs the user of the effort they've put in and how close they are to the finish line

Information architecture

Designing a scaleable and goal-driven long form similar to Airbnb's

The original Facebook carousel ad form had no organizational structure and components were simply appended onto the end with no thought or reason. Thus, the first step was to take into account the constraints of all campaign types in the marketing section and the research I did on Airbnb to establish an organized structure to form components and group them based on the component's purpose.

However, forms would get too unwieldy as more resources were added to the page. This was a non-issue on desktop because of the excess screen real-estate, but mobile couldn't afford to be wasteful. Thus, I came up with a new user flow that was inspired by Airbnb's goal oriented flow. To do this, it would leverage the strengths of the mobile medium, such as drill-downs and tap-ins.

Accessible preview

Giving the merchant power to see their progress

Merchant's favourite feature of Facebook carousel ad creation was the accessible dynamic preview. They could see their progress and how editing each form element would affect the way their ad would look live on Facebook. However, mobile web essentially killed the functionality of this feature due to it being pushed to the bottom of the form. So, one of the main features of this redesign was moving preview into a sticky footer button; merchants could once again keep track of their ad creation progress on mobile.

Feedback

Receiving feedback early and iterating upon ideas

I learned early on how important it is to get feedback early from those with more experience. Not only did I book meetings with senior designers for their perspectives, I also reached out to developers early on. Therefore, I was able to solve issues for both UX and eng early on. This project was a huge lesson in understanding the importance of feedback for my own design process.

Impact

Revitalizing Shopify's mobile design

The mobile native marketing section redesign is now in the build phase. Since no team within Shopify had touched the mobile design system in a while, I was able to introduce some new patterns to our mobile design system.

Learnings from these 12 weeks
